\section{Carrier Contract and Parameter Matching}
\label{app:carriers}

With frozen encoder $E$, frozen predictor/output map $P$, latent $z_t=E(o_t)$, standardized action block $a_{t-1}$, and carrier state $m_{t-1}$,
\begin{equation}
  (b_t,m_t,\widetilde z_t)=C_\phi(m_{t-1},z_t,a_{t-1}),
  \qquad
  \widehat z_{t+1}=P(\widetilde z_{t-2:t},a_{t-2:t}).
\end{equation}
Only $\phi$ is optimized. The target is the cached raw next latent, not a carrier-fused latent. Fixed-trust memory predicts a channelwise prior from state and action, applies a learned input-independent trust to the current latent innovation, and returns a residual read to LeWM space. The GRU, LSTM, and diagonal state-space carriers use the same timing and fusion path.

\subsection{Adaptive origin and fresh inference}

Two broader matched-host screens were sealed before this study.  Both stopped
globally at their declared prerequisite gates: color was the unique target that
passed at every age on both Reacher and PushT, whereas location failed at every
age on both hosts and TwoRoom did not enter carrier training.  We consequently
locked the deterministic rule ``retain color as the target and exact-balance
location as a nuisance'' before constructing any new bank or observing any new
carrier outcome.  This makes the study admission-informed rather than a fresh
confirmatory test of target selection.  All carrier inference, however, uses
new banks and no metric from either stopped screen.

Reacher uses new simulator seeds disjoint from both screens.  PushT excludes
the union of every earlier HDF5 episode index before applying its new split
permutation.  Each host has 1,200 training and 480 validation episodes, one
20-frame sequence per episode, and disjoint train/validation episodes.  Labels
are independent of the base trajectory.  All 16 color--location combinations
are exactly balanced in each split.  The four RGB colors, normalized cue
centers, and square size are identical across hosts.

The cue occupies three observations.  Its intervals are 12--14, 8--10, and
1--3 for ages 4, 8, and 15 at the common decision index 19.  The endpoint is
$[z_{16},z_{17},z_{18},h^-_{19}]$; observation 19 is excluded.  For each host,
arm, and seed, one label-free carrier trains on an equal-age mixture.  A
separate training-only logistic readout is fitted per age.  There are five
carrier seeds per arm and 50 formal cells in total.

The paired bootstrap jointly resamples carrier seeds across hosts, resamples
episodes independently within host, and stratifies episodes by the 16-way
color--location label.  Ages and arms remain paired within host.  The primary
interaction is the PushT-minus-Reacher difference in the age-15
fixed-trust-minus-state-space contrast.  The equivalence margin is $\pm.05$;
the two-one-sided test therefore uses a 90\% interval.  We separately report a
95\% interval for the nonzero-effect question.  Each cell also preserves
per-location and worst-location accuracy.

Table~\ref{tab:appendix-matched-color} reports every host--carrier--age cell,
the paired age decline, and the registered interaction.  Seed arrays,
worst-location records, all 50 cell identities, and the independent
recomputation receipt remain in the machine-readable manifest rather than an
experiment-log dump.

\section{Persistent Spatial Carriers on DINO-WM PushT}
\label{app:dinowm-carrier}

Unlike the diagnostic-only imagination precursor in
Appendix~\ref{app:dinowm-precursor}, this study trains and compares persistent
carrier sidecars on the frozen host.
The host is the original official proprioceptive DINO-WM PushT checkpoint and
its native OSF \texttt{pusht\_noise} bank.  This bank is not matched to LeWM and
must not support a numerical cross-family ranking.  The frozen modules are the
DINOv2 encoder, predictor, action encoder, and proprioceptive encoder.  Their
combined state digest is checked before and after every cell.

Each observation yields 196 visual tokens of width 384.  A single
parameter-shared carrier operates independently along all 196 patch
trajectories; the official frozen predictor remains the only cross-patch
mixing operation.  The residual carrier output replaces only native visual
channels.  Native proprioceptive and action channels are unchanged.  GRU,
LSTM, state-space, and fixed-trust arms contain 298,368, 299,632, 299,520, and
299,520 trainable parameters, respectively; the no-state arm contains zero.
The objective is
the frozen native shifted next-feature MSE on visual and proprioceptive targets,
with no task label.

Token recall has four labels: a red, green, blue, or amber cue colors both the
frame border and a square in its upper-left corner.  Binding recall has six
labels: the six permutations of three ordered red/green/blue swatches, followed
by one label-invariant neutral anchor.  Only these declared cue pixels differ
across counterfactuals; the underlying native frame and every post-cue input
remain identical.

Token and binding cues occupy observations 1--3.  Reads occur at endpoint
frames 7, 11, and 18, giving ages 4, 8, and 15 while excluding the endpoint
observation.  The primary representation is the frozen predictor's final visual
output under full episode state, pooled at spatial levels $1,2,4$.  The reset
control reinitializes the identical trained carrier at the first frame of each
legal three-frame context.  A gain over no state measures the total carrier
effect; a gain over reset estimates, under this intervention, the contribution
of state accumulated before that local context.  Strong persistent retention
requires both paired 95\% intervals to lie above zero.

The formal grid contains two tasks, five arms, five seeds, and three ages (50
formal cells; ages share a cell).  It uses 1,200 training and 480 validation
episodes.  Inference is a 20,000-draw matched-seed and class-stratified
held-out-episode bootstrap.  A sealed numerical amendment changed only the
cache-layout tolerance after the parent attempt stopped before carrier
outcomes: exact original-layout replay remained bit-identical, and the reusable
full-bank cache drift was bounded below $10^{-4}$ with zero existing-readout
prediction flips.  Tasks, grid, objective, optimization, endpoints, and
inference were unchanged; the parent stop receipt is preserved.

Table~\ref{tab:appendix-dinowm-pusht} gives all 30 task--age--arm reads and both
paired controls.  At age 15 the mean carrier-prior reads are 66.0/48.0/81.2/
99.2\% for GRU/LSTM/state-space/fixed-trust on token recall and
38.3/33.5/64.2/97.2\% on binding.  Full-state next-feature MSE remains within
.0466--.0598 and does not rank semantic recall.  The manifest binds these
diagnostics, the amendment, all 50 cells, their preserved predictions, and the
independent 20,000-draw recomputation.

\section{DINO-WM PointMaze Retention and External Use}
\label{app:pointmaze}

The host is the official proprioceptive DINO-WM PointMaze checkpoint and its
2,000-episode native trajectory archive.  We choose 300 training and 120
validation base windows from disjoint native episodes.  Each base window is
repeated under all four labels, yielding 1,200/480 sequences while preserving
the native episode as the statistical cluster.  The target is one of four fixed
waypoints.  A transient mini-map card contains a label-invariant U-shaped route
and one position-varying goal dot; no label-specific color is permitted.  Cue
pixels occur only in observations 1--3, and all later pixels, actions,
proprioception, and simulator states are identical across the four variants.

The carrier interface, ages, spatial pyramid, objective, arms, and five-seed
training contract match the DINO-WM PushT extension.  The 20,000-draw paired
bootstrap resamples matched carrier seeds and native validation episodes while
keeping each four-label counterfactual set intact.  We retain absolute
full-state accuracy, full-minus-no-state, full-minus-reset, prior reads, and
next-feature error at ages 4, 8, and 15.

The reset is deliberately diagnostic rather than distribution preserving.
Across ages, mean reset/full next-visual-MSE ratios are 1.15--1.38 for GRU,
1.85--2.40 for LSTM, 31.4--38.7 for state-space, and 32.8--39.1 for
fixed-trust.  A positive full-minus-reset interval therefore shows dependence
on state preceding the legal context under this intervention; it is not an
unconfounded scalar estimate of retained information.  Full-minus-no-state and
external execution are reported separately.

External execution is enabled only after cue, shortcut, frozen-host, and
controller gates pass.  At age 15, one standardized multinomial consumer per
carrier seed is trained on the pooled full-state training features from every
arm.  The deck contains no arm identifier, and the same fitted consumer is
evaluated on the five full-state arm sources.  Prior and reset reads are not
executed.  Its selected waypoint is executed
for 160 steps by the unmodified released PointMaze
\texttt{WaypointController} (source SHA-256 \texttt{d7b2f9a8a101}) under
MuJoCo 3.10.0 and the released XML (SHA-256 \texttt{64d83955572d}).  The oracle
must achieve at least .90
overall and .85 for every goal, off-diagonal false success must not exceed .10,
and deterministic reset replay must equal 1.0.  A resolved execution gain
requires positive paired lower bounds against both no state and the realized
random-goal arm.  This is external memory-conditioned navigation execution,
not native DINO-WM planning.

Tables~\ref{tab:appendix-pointmaze-carriers}--\ref{tab:appendix-pointmaze-use}
report every gate, all 15 carrier reads with both controls, and every external
consumer outcome.  The machine-readable manifest binds the native episode
clusters, carrier seed arrays, five independently refitted consumer coefficient
digests, execution deck, all 25 cells, and both audit receipts.

\section{Heterogeneous-Cue Fixed-Endpoint Precursor}
\label{app:cue-age}

This precursor retains different cue semantics across tasks; the matched-color primary comparison is reported in Appendix~\ref{app:matched-primary}. The cross-checkpoint comparison changes task, visuals, dynamics, and delay together. The strict branch intervenes on cue placement within each task by holding the decision index and underlying episode trajectory fixed while translating only the cue overlay. Reacher always reads before observation 63 at 15, 24, 32, or 43 observations after the cue; PushT always reads before observation 19 at 4, 8, or 15 observations after the cue. The final raw-latent window, actions, simulator state, label, and base episode are identical across ages. Absolute cue time and cue background co-vary with the offset. Reacher regeneration must exactly match the archived source arrays before cue translation; PushT retains the same official episode and local-start identity and permits mutations only inside declared cue pixels.

For each task, module, and seed, one memory module is trained on a mixture containing every training episode once at every registered age, with equal age weight. The same held-out base episode is then evaluated at every age. This avoids fitting a different module to each delay; a separate prespecified linear readout is nevertheless fitted on the training split at each age. All learned modules use five seeds, the frozen encoder/predictor and 100-epoch training schedule remain fixed, and the readout excludes the decision observation. Paired 95\% intervals use 20,000 bootstrap draws over checkpoint seed and episode, with class-stratified episode resampling for PushT. Task admission and final evaluation use the same locked validation bank, so inference is conditional on that bank; admission is evaluated before memory training and does not select a module or age.

\section{Legacy Claim Boundary}

For the heterogeneous-cue, context, rollout, and repair studies above, the
evidence establishes: (i) frozen cue encoding for four categorical tasks;
(ii) causal final-read carrier comparisons under two LeWM checkpoints;
(iii) robustness to additional Reacher seeds and fresh banks; (iv) a
fixed-endpoint cue-timing intervention; (v) a raw-context/predictor-output
dissociation; (vi) external-consumer execution effects on Reacher color and
PushT token, plus relative PushT binding sensitivity; (vii) partial diagnostic
portability to native DINO-WM imagination; and (viii) two unsuccessful
label-free repairs.  The controlled matched and persistent DINO-WM studies
have separate task-level claims and receipt-bound tables.

It does not establish native consumption of state by a released LeWM planner, end-to-end memory-conditioned control, policy optimality, simultaneous multi-item capacity, natural-scene memory, universal carrier rankings, or carrier generalization across world-model families.
